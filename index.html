1 · Executive Summary Survey Context, Headline Findings & Strategic Implications
A. Survey Context & Scope
Survey TitleYouth Aspiration Survey (YAS) — Hyderabad
Conducting OrganizationForward Trust (Lighthouse Initiative)
Survey PeriodFebruary 5 – February 18, 2026
Target PopulationYouth aged 18–35 in peri-urban communities around Borabanda
Sampling MethodCommunity-based household enumeration
Total Valid Responses1,254 (of 1,612 rows; 358 are blank/metadata)
Surveyors Deployed76 field surveyors
Communities Covered~30+ communities; primary: Borabanda, Site 3, Rajiv Gandhi Nagar, Mothi Nagar
Survey InstrumentKoboToolbox / ODK — 232 columns across demographics, employment, aspirations, skilling
Primary ObjectiveMap youth employment status, skill gaps, aspirations, willingness for Govt skilling programs
C. Strategic Implications
  • Prioritize Digital & Office skilling tracks — highest stated demand, clear employment pathways (BPO, KPO, retail admin)
  • Address information asymmetry first — 55.8% unaware of vocational options; targeted IEC campaigns can unlock latent demand
  • Borabanda Lighthouse is optimally positioned — ~91.7% willing to travel there; collocate skilling + placement services
  • Design gender-sensitive pathways — Beauty Parlour, Fashion Designing, and BPO tracks show female-skewed uptake
  • Align salary expectations — misalignment between ₹25K+ expectations and ₹15–20K market rates risks post-placement dropout
  • Embed job security framing in marketing — emphasize ESIC, PF, and permanent employment outcomes
B. Headline Findings 10 Key Insights
#1
Employment Crisis
62.3% are unemployed but willing to work — a substantial latent labour pool in high-density peri-urban communities.
#2
Education Profile
Predominantly 12th pass (36.6%) and 10th pass (22.9%); only 20.3% are graduates or above.
#3
Skill Gap Acknowledged
Only 7.5% have ever enrolled in vocational training. 55.8% were unaware such courses existed.
#4
Salary Mismatch
35.4% expect >₹25,000/month; median HH income is ₹20–25K — expectations are near-ceiling.
#5
High Skilling Appetite
248 respondents (34.9%) are very willing to join Government skilling programs; 72% are willing or above.
#6
Top Skill Demand
Digital Marketing (339), Office Executive (327), and Account Executive (259) are the top 3 preferred courses.
#7
Aspiration vs. Reality Gap
"Any office work" & Health Sector top aspirations; yet 57% of employed work in blue-collar roles.
#8
Job Security Dominates
Job Security (759 mentions) and Earning Potential (586) are the two most critical job selection factors.
#9
Proximity Constraint
62.3% unwilling to travel more than 30–60 min; 91.7% would travel to Borabanda Lighthouse.
#10
Gender Disparity
Females: 11.4% "not allowed to work" vs 3.3% male. Higher household responsibility burden.

7.3 Analytical Methodology
ItemDetail
Scope FilterAnalysis restricted to 1,254 valid respondents (Q.8 Gender not null). 358 blank rows excluded.
Community StandardizationString matching applied to consolidate ~228 raw variants into ~30 canonical names via regex rules.
Multi-select HandlingBinary (0/1) columns per option; sum across gives mention count. Percentages vs. responding n, not total N.
Missing Data TreatmentDNA and DK excluded from percentage denominators. Noted explicitly where exclusion affects interpretation.
Employment Split3 segments: Employed (n=288), Unemployed-Willing (n=781), Unemployed-Unwilling (n=148). DNA (n=37) excluded.
Budget FrameworkFirst-principles using demand signals mapped to standard NGO/social-enterprise cost structures. Indicative only.
Descriptive vs. InferentialAll analysis is descriptive/exploratory. No hypothesis testing — cross-sectional, convenience-sampled survey.
